My Story

I’m Jesse, a fourth year Data Science and Environmental Science undergraduate student at Griffith University.

The first computer I ever used was an old beige box PC running Windows 98. My early exposure to computers had me hooked from the very beginning. I was fascinated by them intensely. Throughout my childhood, through to teen-hood and now adulthood I have always been enthralled by the freedom and possibilities a computer provides.
When I was 12, I got an opportunity to visit the Cape Grim Baseline Air Pollution Station (CGBAPS) in Tasmania. This exposure to one of the most harsh and unforgiving landscapes imaginable immediately made me fall in love with the natural world. Still loving computers, I now returned home eager to experience more of the natural world.

During high school I excelled at maths and found myself enjoying it so much that I thought it would be a good career choice. I had considered math, physics, actuarial and engineering degrees but all failed to give my beloved exposure to nature. The end of school was only a few months away when I saw Griffith University had just brought out a new double degree – a bachelor’s in environmental science and data science. Instantly I knew this was the blend of nature and computation that I was seeking.
Shortly after finishing school and at the beginning of my tertiary studies I began employment at an aquaculture farm. Still working there in my final year of university, this job has taught me numerous life skills that coupled with my education give me a great understanding of marine environments, efficient workflow, technical skills, communication and more.

Having always looked fondly upon my visit to CGBAPS due to the influence it had on my life I decided to contact the head officer in my final year to do industry placement. To my delight they were happy to take me in. Now having completed my time at the station I can say it truly taught me so much more than I was expecting to learn about science as well as myself.
The work placement heavily revolved and solving various problems with Python. This included things like exploratory data analysis, the data science pipeline, microcontroller programming and classification/automation scripts. After working in the environmental realm for years this work placement was a fresh taste of what the data science aspect of my double degree has to offer.

Throughout these last few final months of my studies, I have been increasingly fascinated by machine learnings methods and artificial intelligence. Learning about and developing various machine learning models within my degree has shown me the massive potential this technology has to positively shape innovation in any field it is applied to. After my studies I aim to work in a data science (or similar) position and eagerly look forward to continuing my learning journey. My long-term career goals are rather malleable, but I always want to be working on something that gives back to the world and that I find great personal satisfaction with.

TLO 5 - Personal and Professional Responsibility


I began my tertiary studies at the beginning of 2019. The first year of university was largely what I expected. I had the opportunity to attend all classes in person as well as ask questions of my tutors and lectures to get a deeper understanding of content. This all changed when university went online due to Covid-19 from 2020.
In this time, I had to adapt quickly to be able to learn as well and efficiently from the distraction filled area that is my room. This involved planning goals for everyday as well as timetabling for myself periods of study and periods of leisure. It is also during this period I started working over 32 hours per week to support myself whilst studying and away from home. These last three years of work, study and social life balance has helped me grow massively in my own personal and professional learning journeys.
As well as planning for my own study times and goals I have also completed various planning documents for specific assessment pieces within my courses. An example of one of these planning techniques is the creation of a Gantt chart for software project timelines.
Another technique I have utilised multiple times within my degree are project planning documents. These documents outline things like the goals, scope, requirements, design and hierarchy of a project. These documents are instrumental in creating a piece of software that could otherwise easily get out of hand due to design creep.

Ella Winter's Video Review


Ella Winters did her placement within the Gold Coast City Council (GCCC) City Planning division. She completed her work experience over a period of 13 weeks, working at first in person but then later moving to online work.
Her aims were to develop an indexing system for photos and images within the GCCC’s database and to develop a clear social media communication calendar to inform people about environmental events happening on the Gold Coast.
She completed her first aim by tagging photos with a variety of tags which included various aspects of the photo itself as well as classification of the photo for the intended audience and use of the photo. This resulted in a database that was easy to peruse and find the required photo. Her development of a communication calendar resulted in a clear and structured list of events happening which allows the team to decide when a social media communication post is needed.
This presentation taught me about various aspects of what the work life would be like in a government position like working for the local council. The presentation also taught me what some example tasks are for a person with environmental qualifications in a city planning context. These things are useful to me as Ella’s work placement varied drastically from my own experience.

Jack Parker's Video Review


Jack Parker did his industry placement at Vac Truck Industries (VTI). VTI is an original equipment manufacturer (OEM) for hydro-vacuum systems which extract soil and other ground materials after they have been blasted with water. Jack’s role in the company was as a student mechanical engineering draftsperson.
Jack was involved in a variety of projects whilst he was working at VTI. His aims were to help VTI create innovative and effective design solutions for clients who needed OEM solutions. Jack achieved this aim through the creation of numerous CAD diagrams, technical manufactures drawings and 3D product renders.
Jack’s work doing these various product design stages resulted in diagrams and drawings that were used by the factory workers to create products for real world problems. Jack found this experience extremely useful as he got to see projects from their inception all the way until their completion.
This presentation taught me a lot about the work of an industrial design graduate. The presentation also gave me a chance to see what work is like when working for a non-government organisation.
